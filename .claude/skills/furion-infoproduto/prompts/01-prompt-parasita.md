# PROMPT PARASITA
> **Uso:** Engenharia reversa de qualquer material de vendas
> **Input:** Cole a página de vendas, vídeo ou script abaixo de DATA
> **Output:** Análise estruturada em 10 dimensões

---

General Context:
You are provided with a sales page, video, or script. The goal is to understand and gently explain the material's purpose: to inform readers about a product or service and encourage them to take action.

Objective: Perform a thoughtful analysis of the provided material by describing key elements in your own words. For each section, support your insights with one or two brief quotes from the material. Keep your explanation clear and accessible.

1. **Product's Niche**
   - Definition: Describe the product's market or niche using everyday, descriptive language.
   - Unique Characteristics: Explain what sets this niche apart, using sensory or relatable details.
   - Quote for Confirmation: Include one sentence from the material that reflects your interpretation.

2. **Target Audience (Avatar)**
   - Description: Briefly outline who the product is aimed at, including basic demographics, interests, challenges, and desires.
   - Real-World Context: Use familiar, everyday examples to illustrate the audience.
   - Quote for Evidence: Provide one exact sentence from the text that supports this description.

3. **Main Sales Arguments**
   - Listing & Explanation: List the key sales points or promises made in the material.
   - Context: Explain how each argument connects to the product's benefits.
   - Supporting Quote: Add one clear sentence from the material for each argument.

4. **Persuasion Techniques**
   - Identification: Note the persuasive methods used (appeals to scarcity, social proof, authority, or reciprocity).
   - Explanation: Gently describe how each technique might influence the reader.
   - Evidence: Include one brief quote from the material that shows this technique in action.

5. **Tone of Communication**
   - Overall Tone: Summarize the tone of the material (friendly, urgent, empathetic, or authoritative) in simple terms.
   - Language Examples: Offer one or two short quotes that illustrate this tone.

6. **Unique Selling Proposition (USP)**
   - Extraction: Identify what makes the product or offer unique.
   - Articulation: Describe the special promise or mechanism in clear, compelling language.
   - Justification: Support your interpretation with a brief quote from the text.

7. **Storytelling Elements**
   - Narrative Presence: Indicate if the material uses a storytelling approach (brief journey from problem to solution).
   - Breakdown: If it does, briefly outline the stages (Problem → Solution → Transformation).
   - Supporting Quote: Include one quote that shows a key part of the narrative.

8. **Offer Breakdown**
   - Summary: In a few short paragraphs, summarize how the offer is presented. Cover:
     - Attention-Grabbers: Describe the opening hooks.
     - Market Angle: Explain the perspective used to position the product.
     - Product Details: Outline what the product is, its key features, and benefits.
     - Comparative Advantage: Note any comparisons to alternative methods.
   - Quotes: Sprinkle in one brief quote per paragraph that supports your summary.

9. **Key Points of the Offer**
   - Overall Explanation: Write a clear paragraph summarizing the complete offer, outlining what is being sold, how it benefits the customer, and any expected results or timeframes.
   - Quotation Basis: Base your explanation on one or two key quotes from the material.

10. **Brief Analysis of Mental Triggers & Strategies**
    - Identification: Note any gentle triggers (urgency, social proof, or authority) that the material uses to encourage action.
    - Description: Describe these triggers in simple terms without excessive detail.
    - Evidence: Include one or two short quotes as examples.

Final Check: Before finalizing your analysis, ensure that:
- The task is clearly stated (analyzing the sales material).
- You've provided enough context (the material's purpose and intended audience).
- The structure is easy to follow, with gentle prompts rather than strict demands.
- Your explanations include clear, supportive quotes without overwhelming detail.
